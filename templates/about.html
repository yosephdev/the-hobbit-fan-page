About Page

Thorin and Company was an organization led by Thorin Oakenshield and created by Gandalf the Grey. They were initially thirteen Dwarves, all but three part of the Line of Durin. However, they were later joined by the Hobbit Bilbo Baggins. They succeeded in their quest to reclaim Erebor from the dragon Smaug and fought alongside the Dwarves of the Iron Hills against Azog's and Sauron's forces. However, only ten survived the Battle of the Five Armies, with Thorin and his nephews Fíli and Kíli falling in battle.

Thorin Oakenshield

Thorin is the exiled king of the Dwarves of Erebor, driven from their homeland by the dragon Smaug. He leads a company of dwarves, aided by the wizard Gandalf and hobbit Bilbo Baggins, on a quest to reclaim the Lonely Mountain and slay Smaug. Initially distrustful of Bilbo, Thorin grows to respect him after witnessing his bravery. A skilled warrior and born leader, Thorin wields the dwarven sword Deathless and later the elvish blade Orcrist. He bears scars from his battle with the pale orc Azog, who had previously led the attack on Erebor. Determined to restore his people's home, Thorin's single-minded pursuit of this goal drives the company's epic journey across Middle-earth, personifying the dwarves' unwavering resolve.

Fili and Kili

Fíli and Kíli were nephews of Thorin, who was the elder brother of their mother Dís. They were also nephews of Frerin, grandsons of Thráin II, and great-grandsons of Thrór. Fíli's most distinguishing feature was his long nose, the longest of any of the Dwarves in Thorin's company.
