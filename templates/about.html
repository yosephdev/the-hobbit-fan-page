About Page

Thorin and Company was an organization led by Thorin Oakenshield and created by Gandalf the Grey. They were initially thirteen Dwarves, all but three part of the Line of Durin. However, they were later joined by the Hobbit Bilbo Baggins. They succeeded in their quest to reclaim Erebor from the dragon Smaug and fought alongside the Dwarves of the Iron Hills against Azog's and Sauron's forces. However, only ten survived the Battle of the Five Armies, with Thorin and his nephews Fíli and Kíli falling in battle.

Thorin Oakenshield

Thorin is the exiled king of the Dwarves of Erebor, driven from their homeland by the dragon Smaug. He leads a company of dwarves, aided by the wizard Gandalf and hobbit Bilbo Baggins, on a quest to reclaim the Lonely Mountain and slay Smaug. Initially distrustful of Bilbo, Thorin grows to respect him after witnessing his bravery. A skilled warrior and born leader, Thorin wields the dwarven sword Deathless and later the elvish blade Orcrist. He bears scars from his battle with the pale orc Azog, who had previously led the attack on Erebor. Determined to restore his people's home, Thorin's single-minded pursuit of this goal drives the company's epic journey across Middle-earth, personifying the dwarves' unwavering resolve.

Fili and Kili

Fíli and Kíli were nephews of Thorin, who was the elder brother of their mother Dís. They were also nephews of Frerin, grandsons of Thráin II, and great-grandsons of Thrór. Fíli's most distinguishing feature was his long nose, the longest of any of the Dwarves in Thorin's company. Kíli was a skilled archer and used a longbow as his main weapon during the quest to Erebor.

Óin

One of the oldest dwarves in the company, Óin was a descendant of the royal line of Durin. He was a skilled healer and carried an ear trumpet due to his diminished hearing. Óin fought valiantly in the Battle of Azanulbizar against the orcs.


Glóin

The son of Gróin and brother of Óin, Glóin was known for his fiery red hair and beard. He was married to an unnamed dwarrowdam and had a son named Gimli, who later became part of the Fellowship of the Ring.


Balin

A wise and experienced dwarf lord, Balin was one of Thorin's closest advisors. He had previously lived in Erebor before the coming of Smaug. Balin's knowledge of the Lonely Mountain's layout proved invaluable during the quest.


Dwalin

A formidable warrior and Balin's younger brother, Dwalin was Thorin's trusted friend and fighter. With his bald head and intricate tattoos, Dwalin cut an intimidating figure and was always ready for battle.


Ori

The youngest of the Ri brothers, Ori was a skilled scribe who documented the company's journey in his journal. Despite his youth, he proved his bravery time and again during the quest.


Dori

The eldest Ri brother, Dori was a worrier who fretted over the safety of his younger siblings Nori and Ori. However, he was also a capable fighter when the situation called for it.


Nori

The middle Ri brother, Nori had a mischievous streak and a knack for getting into trouble. Despite his roguish ways, he was fiercely loyal to his brothers and the company's cause.


Bifur

Bifur had a piece of an orc's axe embedded in his forehead, which caused him to speak only in Khuzdul. Despite this, he was a skilled warrior and toymaker who contributed his unique talents to the quest.


Bofur

The cheerful and good-natured Bofur was a skilled miner and often provided comic relief to the company. He wore a floppy hat and carried a mattock, which he used as a weapon when needed.


Bombur

The largest and most rotund of the dwarves, Bombur was a skilled cook and a loyal member of the company. Despite his size, he proved his worth time and again during the perilous journey.


Gandalf

The wizard Gandalf the Grey was the one who instigated the quest to reclaim Erebor and recruited Bilbo Baggins as the company's "burglar." He guided and protected the dwarves throughout their journey, using his magic and wisdom to aid them in their quest.
